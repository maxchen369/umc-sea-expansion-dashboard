# UMC Southeast Asia Semiconductor Landscape Dashboard — Thesis

## 1. Purpose
This dashboard is designed to organize the semiconductor landscape across Southeast Asia in the context of UMC’s existing foundry presence in Singapore.

It is not intended to recommend whether UMC should build a new fab, enter a new country, or pursue a specific expansion strategy.

Its purpose is to create a clear information flow that helps management understand:
- what semiconductor activity exists in each Southeast Asian country;
- where each country sits in the semiconductor value chain;
- what infrastructure and talent constraints matter;
- which company cases are meaningful;
- which policy signals are relevant but not yet actionable;
- where public data is still insufficient.

The dashboard supports strategy discussion, but strategy judgment remains with human decision-makers.

## 2. Context
UMC already has an established foundry presence in Singapore. Therefore, Singapore should be treated as the existing regional anchor and reference point, not simply as one country candidate in a ranking exercise.

The dashboard should focus on understanding the broader Southeast Asian semiconductor landscape around this existing Singapore presence.

## 3. Core Question
Given UMC’s existing Singapore foundry presence, what is the current semiconductor landscape across Southeast Asia, and how can the relevant country facts, ecosystem evidence, infrastructure constraints, company cases, policy signals, and data gaps be organized into a useful management information flow?

## 4. What This Dashboard Is
This dashboard is:
- an information map;
- a country and ecosystem fact base;
- a semiconductor value-chain overview;
- a case library;
- a policy and infrastructure signal tracker;
- a data-gap tracker;
- a discussion aid for management.

## 5. What This Dashboard Is Not
This dashboard is not:
- an investment recommendation engine;
- a country ranking model;
- a scoring model;
- a replacement for strategy judgment;
- a ROIC model;
- a site selection model;
- a decision to build or not build a fab.

## 6. Organizing Logic
The dashboard should organize information through five lenses:
1. Country Landscape  
   What semiconductor activity exists in each country?
2. Value Chain Position  
   Is the country stronger in wafer fabrication, packaging and test, IC design, equipment/materials, EMS/electronics demand, or policy/talent development?
3. Infrastructure & Talent Conditions  
   What are the observable constraints around electricity, reliability, materials, engineering talent, salary, and industrial clusters?
4. Company & Policy Cases  
   Which company cases and policy programs are meaningful, and what do they actually show?
5. Data Gaps  
   Which information remains unavailable or requires internal UMC validation?

## 7. Treatment of Strategy
The dashboard may help users think about possible strategic directions, but it should not generate the strategy itself.

Possible strategic implications may be noted as “discussion prompts,” but they should not be presented as recommendations.

For example:
- Malaysia’s packaging and OSAT ecosystem may be relevant to future partnership discussions.
- Vietnam’s EMS and electronics base may be relevant to customer-support discussions.
- Thailand’s automotive and power electronics base may be relevant to mature-node demand monitoring.
- Indonesia’s ARM / Danantara case may be relevant as a design-talent and policy signal.
- The Philippines’ ATP base may be relevant to back-end or support discussions.

These are discussion prompts, not recommendations.

## 8. Output
The dashboard should produce four outputs:
1. Southeast Asia Country Fact Base  
   Objective country-level data and key qualitative facts.
2. Semiconductor Value Chain Map  
   Where each country sits across wafer fabrication, packaging/test, IC design, equipment/materials, EMS/electronics, policy/talent.
3. Case & Policy Library  
   Structured cases with facts, relevance, limitation, and source trail.
4. Data Gap Tracker  
   Missing information that requires further research or internal validation.

## 9. Design Principle
The dashboard should prioritize clarity over complexity.

It should help the reader move from:
raw data → structured information → discussion-ready insight.

It should not move from:
raw data → subjective score → artificial recommendation.
